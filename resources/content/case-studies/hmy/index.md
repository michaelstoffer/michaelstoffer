## Overview

HMY Yacht Sales is one of the largest yacht brokerage companies in the world, with a large inventory of new and pre-owned vessels, multiple locations, and a customer base that expects a premium experience at every touchpoint.

A website in this space isn't just marketing — it's a core part of the sales process. Listings need to be accurate, fast to load, and easy to update. The editorial team needs to move quickly without waiting on developers. And the overall experience needs to match the caliber of what's being sold.

When I came on to the project, the existing setup wasn't meeting any of those needs particularly well. I designed and built a custom Laravel CMS and Vue.js frontend to replace it.

---

## The Problem

The legacy system made routine content work harder than it needed to be. Updating listings, managing media, and making editorial changes required too much friction — either developer involvement, a clunky interface, or both.

On the frontend, performance was suffering. For a site where users are browsing high-resolution imagery of multi-million dollar yachts, slow load times and a dated UI weren't just an inconvenience — they were a liability.

The core needs were:

<ul>
    <li>A CMS built around HMY's actual content model — inventory, listings, locations, editorial pages</li>
    <li>An editorial interface the team could use confidently without developer hand-holding</li>
    <li>A modern, fast frontend that reflected the quality of the brand</li>
    <li>A maintainable codebase that wouldn't become a burden to own long-term</li>
</ul>

---

## What I Built

The platform is a custom Laravel application with a Vue.js frontend styled with Tailwind CSS — built specifically around HMY's workflows rather than adapted from a generic CMS.

<strong>Key features included:</strong>

<ul>
    <li><strong>Custom Laravel CMS</strong> — Built from scratch around HMY's content model, including inventory management, listing pages, media handling, and editorial content</li>
    <li><strong>Vue.js + Tailwind frontend</strong> — A modern, component-driven UI designed to be fast, clean, and consistent across the site</li>
    <li><strong>Editorial autonomy</strong> — The team can manage listings, update content, and publish pages without touching code or filing requests</li>
    <li><strong>Performance improvements</strong> — Cleaner architecture, optimized queries, and a leaner frontend stack replaced the overhead of the previous system</li>
    <li><strong>Maintainable foundation</strong> — Straightforward Laravel conventions and a well-structured Vue component library make the codebase easy to extend and own</li>
</ul>

## The Outcome

The result is a platform that works the way HMY's team actually works. Content updates that used to require developer time now happen directly through the CMS. The frontend is faster, cleaner, and more representative of the brand. And the codebase is something that can be maintained and extended without turning into a long-term headache.

This is one of the projects I'm most proud of — not because of the technology stack, but because it's a clear example of understanding a real business context, building something purposeful around it, and delivering something that keeps working.
